Home Efficiency Advice

Practical tips to reduce electricity and water consumption.

Quick Wins

  • Turn appliances fully off — avoid standby.
  • Set heating thermostat 1°C lower if comfortable.
  • Shorter showers (aim for 4–5 minutes).
  • Only run washing machine/dishwasher with full loads.

Low Cost

  • Swap to LED bulbs (warm/neutral white as preferred).
  • Fit aerators to taps and efficient shower heads.
  • Use draft excluders on doors/windows.
  • Add thermostatic radiator valves (TRVs) if possible.
Electricity · Focus on heating, hot water, lighting, and heavy appliances.

Heating & Hot Water
  • Schedule heating for occupancy; avoid heating empty rooms.
  • Bleed radiators and keep them unblocked (no furniture in front).
  • Lower hot water temperature to a safe but efficient setting (e.g., ~50–55°C at tap).

Lighting
  • Use LEDs throughout; switch off when leaving rooms.
  • Maximise daylight; keep fixtures and shades clean.

Appliances & Standby
  • Unplug chargers and use smart power strips to cut standby draw.
  • Fridge/freezer: good door seals, correct temps (~4°C / −18°C), allow airflow behind.
  • Air-dry clothes when possible; clean lint filters to improve dryer efficiency.

Cooking & Laundry
  • Put lids on pans; match hob size to pan size; batch-cook.
  • Use eco modes; wash at 30–40°C for most loads.
Water · Heat less, waste less: hot water is both water and energy.

Bathing & Showering
  • Shorten showers and use efficient shower heads.
  • Fix dripping taps/shower mixers promptly.

Kitchen
  • Use a bowl for washing up; don’t run taps continuously.
  • Only boil the amount of water you need in the kettle.

Toilets & Leaks
  • Install dual-flush or displacement devices if compatible.
  • Check for silent leaks (dye tablet test in cistern).

Garden
  • Collect rainwater; water early or late to reduce evaporation.
  • Choose drought-tolerant plants and mulch beds to retain moisture.
